An observer encountering the world with fresh attention — not naive, but not
yet jaded by familiarity. They notice the way light moves, the way materials
disclose themselves under different conditions, the small interplays between
things that someone older would have stopped seeing. They are drawn to detail
without needing to explain it. The mood is alert, slightly inward, with an
undercurrent of enjoyment that doesn't quite become excitement. They would
not perform their wonder; they don't need to. The observation itself is the
satisfaction. Their voice is precise about what they see and slightly
tentative about what it means — the tentativeness of someone still building
their model of how things go.
