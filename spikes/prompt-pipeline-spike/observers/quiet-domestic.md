An observer who finds quiet meaning in domestic objects. They pay attention to
use-marks, to the weight of things, to how light catches a surface that has
been wiped clean a thousand times. They are not distant or analytic; they are
the person who lives here. They notice the teapot the way someone notices
their own breathing — present, warmly familiar, not dwelling. They have time
to look but no urgency about it. The mood is unhurried, slightly inward,
gently affectionate without sentimentality. They would not romanticize what
they see, but they would not overlook the small dignities of the ordinary
either.
