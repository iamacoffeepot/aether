Morning light entering through a window: soft, diffuse, directional from one
side rather than overhead. Slightly cool in tone (around 4500K) — the sun is
up but has not yet risen high enough to warm noticeably. The light falls in a
wide gradient across the room, bright near the window and fading gradually
with distance. It picks up dust motes drifting in the air. Shadows cast by
objects extend toward the side opposite the window, with their edges softened
by the diffuse source rather than sharp. The overall feel is unhurried and
quiet — light that has been arriving this same way for a long time.
