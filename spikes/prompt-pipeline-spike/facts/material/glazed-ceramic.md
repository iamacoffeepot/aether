Glazed ceramic: a clay body fired at high temperature with a glassy mineral
coating fused to its surface. The glaze produces a hard, slightly reflective
finish that catches highlights cleanly along curved surfaces but stays
low-contrast on flat areas. Color sits in the glaze layer rather than the
substrate, so chips reveal the unglazed body underneath. The surface is cool
to the touch and slightly slick; condensation beads in clean droplets rather
than spreading. Sound when struck is bright and short — a clean ring that
decays quickly.
