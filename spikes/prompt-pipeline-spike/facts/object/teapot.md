A classic teapot of the kind formalized in 1975 as a graphics reference object.
The body is a rounded vessel with a gentle taper toward its foot — wider than
tall, with the body's widest point at roughly its vertical mid-height. A single
curved spout rises from one side of the body; opposite it, a single curved
handle. A domed lid sits fitted to the top opening, with a small spherical knob
at the lid's apex. The proportions read as squat and grounded rather than tall
and elegant. The form is functional and immediately legible as "teapot" from
any angle — no ornamental flourish, no decorative relief.
