A small handled cup of the kind that sits beside a teapot. The body is a
rounded bowl, gently tapered from a wider rim toward a slightly narrower
base, sized roughly to a cupped palm. A single small handle loops from one
side, sized for one or two fingers rather than a full grip. A short foot
ring lifts the cup a few millimetres off whatever surface it sits on. The
form is functional and immediately legible as "teacup" — no saucer
implied, no decorative relief, no ornamental flourish.
