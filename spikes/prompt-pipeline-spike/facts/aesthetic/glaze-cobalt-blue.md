Deep cobalt blue glaze. Saturated, rich, leaning slightly toward indigo
in the shadows but unmistakably blue — not turquoise, not navy, not
slate, not teal. Reads as cobalt-blue ceramic of the kind found on
traditional blue-and-white porcelain bodies *minus* any white field
or painted decoration: the entire glazed surface is cobalt, edge to
edge, uniform in hue with the natural depth variation that comes from
glaze pooling slightly thicker in concave areas. Where light catches
the surface, the highlights stay within the cobalt family — a lighter
blue, not a desaturated white. The glaze itself is glossy and
continuous; this fact specifies color only, not surface treatment
(which is governed by the underlying material fact).
